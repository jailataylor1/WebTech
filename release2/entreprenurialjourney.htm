Entrepreneurial Journey

Origin Story

May 2016.

I didn’t think it would be an important day, just another afternoon online. As my eyes stayed fixed on the illuminated computer screen in front of me, I became detached from reality, plunging into the internet realm of accepted beauty standards. I watched videos transfixed, obsessively memorizing the techniques of YouTube ‘beauty gurus’ who used cosmetics to transform their faces into the ‘desired woman.’ Layers of LA Pro Girl color corrector, MAC foundation, and Tarte Shape Tape concealer were effortlessly blended to create the perfect illusion of flawless skin. Over many months, these videos inflamed my insecurities, persuading me to rely on makeup to cover natural imperfections so that society would view me as, hopefully, more beautiful. However, on this day, I was struck by a problem; if my imperfection was my skin complexion how could I possibly conceal that from the world and myself? The women online were all fair, and they only needed to conceal temporary blemishes. Even without makeup, they would still be lucky enough to automatically belong to America’s idealized beauty standards. And, because I am dark-skinned, those standards will always exclude me. This realization totally overwhelmed me and , out of pride and fear, I stifled the shame I felt. But that day, I couldn't stop the tears from staining my cheeks.

Since this day I became hyper aware of my place as an outsider but my facade of indifference never wavered. I even began to fool myself into thinking that ignoring the issue would erase it. Of course, it festered. My values and desires constantly clashed. I realized I had to sacrifice some vanity to truly mature, and abandon social restraints that were holding me back. I worked hard to focus on what made me unique, on internal values instead of superficiality, and to change the way I saw things.

As I finally dethroned these beauty standards, my perception of everything changed. I felt liberated. I no longer belittled myself and my sense of self-disgust evaporated. I began to see the world more critically; many cosmetics suppliers hadn’t been catering to all women, and in providing only a limited shade range, excluded vast portions of society, denying them the dignity that make up can enhance. This realization inspired me to begin developing a women’s fashion line that focuses on body positivity and self love, where my clothing not only accentuates the natural shape of every woman but compliments her elegance and individualistic style. Establishing my brand as affordable yet sustainable, I hope to offer fashion that can be universally accessed by all women while maintaining a social consciousness. Today, as I write this, I am 19, and in the process of maturing, I have moved past my childhood vanities and I am doing the hard work of shaping my future and empowering women around the globe.

Remember: Love yourself always
- Jaila
