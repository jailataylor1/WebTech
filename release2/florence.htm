Florence,
Italy

Florence is an incredble city! As the beating heart of Italy's Tuscan region; Florence, is one of Italy's most famed destinations for good reason. Come to Florence and bask in the city's culmination of Romance and Renaissance in an enchannting anf irresistible way! Art, architecture, and food make up this incredble city so if you are loooking for a gateway into classic Italian culture this city is one you must visit!

Florence is a city compact in size and packed with extraordinary art and architecture masterpieces at every turn. This small city, is also incredbly diverse and sits on the banks of the Arno river in northeastern Tuscany. Since the Renaissance the city has hardly changed and its narrow cobbled streets are a cinematic feast of elegant 15th- and 16th-century buildings, medieval candle-lit chapels, fresco-decorated churches, white-marble basilicas and world-class art museums. This city is brimming with paintings and sculptures by Botticcelli, Michelangelo and all of the classical greats of the modern world. The entire city centre is a Unesco World Heritage Site and when I visited in the summer of 2017 I didn't want to leave.

(Duomo's Across Florence)

What to Expect when Visiting this City!

  1. Language
    • Italian is the official language and it’s good to know a few basics like per favore (please) and grazie (thanks). You’ll find basic English is spoken in tourist areas but don’t expect fluency from everyone you run into.
  2. Currency
    • The primary currency used in Florence is the Euro, like much of Europe!
    • s. Check with your bank before you go to see how conversion in foreign ATMs works and if there’s a charge for using your debit card. In most cases, you’ll find it’s a better rate than old fashion currency exchange offices.
